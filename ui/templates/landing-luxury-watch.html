The House of Aurelian

An obsession with the imperceptible

Each Calibre 01 passes through the hands of a single master watchmaker, from first bridge to final regulation.

I.

Hand Finishing

Every bridge is anglaged by hand, its edges polished to a mirror at forty-five degrees. The black-polished steel takes three days alone.

II.

In-House Movement

The flying tourbillon, escapement, and balance are conceived, machined, and assembled entirely within our Geneva atelier.

III.

Rare Materials

An 18-karat rose gold case, a hand-grained meteorite dial, and a sapphire crystal carved from a single block of crystal.

The Calibre 01

A movement that defies gravity

At its heart turns a flying tourbillon — a rotating cage that averages out the pull of gravity, freed of any upper bridge so it appears to float within the dial.

  • One-minute flying tourbillon with hand-bevelled cage
  • Free-sprung balance running at 21,600 vph
  • Twin barrels delivering a 72-hour reserve
  • Certified to chronometer tolerance in six positions
The Case

Sculpted from solid gold

The 41-millimetre case is machined from a single billet of 18-karat rose gold, its lugs flowing into the wrist with a slimness that belies the complication within.

  • Box-domed sapphire crystal with seven-layer anti-reflective coating
  • Exhibition caseback revealing the finished movement
  • Hand-stitched alligator strap with a gold deployant clasp
  • Water resistant to 30 metres
