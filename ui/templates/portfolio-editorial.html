About

For fifteen years I've worked at the seam where type, image and idea meet — building identities that read like editorials and editorials that behave like brands.

My practice began in the print rooms of small magazines and has since carried me through global rebrands, festival systems, and a stubborn, ongoing love affair with the printed page. I believe restraint is a kind of generosity, and that the right grid can hold an entire personality.

I collaborate closely — with founders, editors, and fellow makers — to find the one idea worth building everything around. The rest is craft, patience, and a great deal of careful spacing.
